Demographic Analysis Insights

  • Age Sweet Spot: Customers in the 30–50 age range represent the highest buying conversion rates.
  • Income Dominance: Annual income shows an exponential correlation with purchase likelihood, particularly above the middle-class threshold.
  • Gender Neutrality: Purchase intent is fairly uniform between genders, with a negligible skew towards male buyers.
  • Market Youth Skew: The Indian demographic profile exhibits a significantly younger median age than the Japanese demographic.

Final Strategic Market Entry Recommendations

1

Proceed with India Market Entry

Both Logistic Regression and Random Forest confirm that sales volume is highly likely to exceed the 12,000 cars/year profitability threshold. Our baseline prediction shows 13,285 potential buyers annually in our sample projection pool, providing a comfortable 10.7% margin buffer.

2

Target High-Income & Middle-Age Groups

Income is the strongest predictor across all models. We should target marketing efforts in metro cities (tier 1 and tier 2) and structure customer segments around age ranges between 30 and 50 years, where buying propensity peaks.

3

Leverage Vehicle Replacement Cycles

The 4-feature models reveal that car owners with vehicles older than 36–48 months (3–4 years) have a significantly higher likelihood of replacement. Marketing campaigns targeting trade-ins and upgrade incentives will yield high conversion rates.

4

Establish Competitive Local Financing

Since income correlates exponentially with buying conversions, offering flexible EMI financing options will lower the barrier to entry for the younger (20-30 years) aspirational segment in India, which shows a rising income path.
